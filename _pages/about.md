## About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Hong Kong Baptist University. My research focuses on artificial intelligence for medical imaging, with a particular interest in 3D ultrasound reconstruction, neural rendering, and Gaussian Splatting.

My current research focuses on 3D reconstruction from 2D ultrasound sequences. I am interested in using AI to build spatial-aware medical image representations. My long-term interest is to integrate medical image reconstruction, 3D representation learning, and human-AI interaction to support more efficient clinical workflows, such as helping clinicians understand scanned regions, assess spatial coverage, and improve the efficiency and reliability of clinical practice.

Previously, I worked on accelerated MRI reconstruction and diffusion-based medical image reconstruction at the Shenzhen Institute of Advanced Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences. I received my B.Eng. degree in Biomedical Engineering from Shenzhen University.
